Yam-Porridge image

Yam porridge is a very delicious Yam recipe
though it’s eaten by most tribes in Nigeria however,
it is more common in the Igbo's-speaking parts of Nigeria.
It’s also easy to make. If you love yam, it’s a must-try recipe!

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 kg (2.2 lbs) white puna yam

  • Red palm Oil (to colour)

  • 1 Smoke Fish(mackerel)

  • 1 tablespoon ground crayfish

  • 1 medium onion

  • Fresh green vegetable (eg pumpkin,
    parsley, scent leaf or green amaranth)

  • Chilli & Habanero pepper & Salt (to taste)

  • 2 big stock cubes

Notes on the ingredients

  1. Add enough red palm oil to make the meal colourful.

  2. You can use dry fish instead of smoked fish.

Before you cook Yam Porridge

  1. Peel and cut the yam tuber into
    medium sizes. Wash the yam cubes and
    place in a sizeable pot.

  2. Wash and cut the onions into tiny pieces.

  3. Wash and cut the onions into
    tiny pieces. Grind or blend the
    chilli pepper. Set these aside.

  4. If using dry fish, soak and pick the bones.

Cooking Directions

  1. Pour enough water to cover the yam
    cubes and start cooking at medium
    to high heat. If you are using dry
    fish, add it now.


  2. When the yams have got a good boil,
    add the onions, ground crayfish,
    pepper, stock cubes, palm oil and
    the smoked fish.


  3. Cover the pot and continue cooking
    till the yam is done.

  4. Add salt to taste and stir very

    5. well. Cook at high heat for about 5
    minutes.

  5. Add the green vegetable, stir and
    leave to stand for about 5 minutes
    then serve.
